Why We Built Our Own Stack

Privacy is not a feature. It's a human right.

Visit ProsperApps ↗

When we looked at how communities would rely on digital tools, we saw a fundamental problem: every mainstream platform (Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, Meta, Uber, Stripe) is engineered to extract data, create dependency, and extract wealth from the communities that use them.

We believe this is not inevitable. The communities we build deserve digital infrastructure that belongs to them. Software without surveillance. Storage without lock-in. Identity without surveillance capitalism.

That's why we invested early and deeply in building ProsperApps, a complete sovereign software stack, aligned with the Foundation's declaration, and designed for the long horizon.

🔒
No surveillance. No data harvesting.

Today's tech giants' business models depend on knowing everything about you. Our model depends on building software people trust. We have no incentive to harvest your data, and we've designed the system to prevent it architecturally.

🔑
You own your identity.

ProsperID and ProsperAuth give users first-party identifiers and passkey-based access. No forced sign-in with Google. No third-party session surveillance. Your identity belongs to you.

📁
Your files stay yours.

ProsperSafe provides browser-encrypted preservation with vault-backed key custody. We literally cannot read your encrypted files. ProsperVault handles secret custody with explicit, governed access.

✉️
Mail you control.

ProsperMail is a privacy-first mail infrastructure, with root-owned delivery and mailbox seams used across the ecosystem. No advertising. No scanning your messages to serve you ads.

🏛️
Foundation-aligned ownership structure.

ProsperApps is structurally tied to the Prosper Foundation's declaration. The business model reinforces long-horizon quality, portability, and trust, not lock-in, extraction, or quality decline over time.

The SaaSpawn method: shared foundations, product specificity.

ProsperApps uses a shared platform backbone (identity, mail, storage, authentication, governance) that all products build on. This is how we achieve the depth of a large software portfolio with the discipline of a focused team. Everything is coherent. Nothing is siloed. Every product makes the next one better.

Why Prosper Drive Exists

The gig economy is the clearest case of extraction over dignity.

~5M

US driving jobs threatened by autonomous vehicles by early 2030s

25–40%

Average platform commission taken by Uber and Lyft on every ride

Nearly five million Americans work in driving occupations. As autonomous vehicle technology matures, these are among the most vulnerable jobs to automation, and the workers facing displacement have built their livelihoods, their families, and their communities on these earnings.

Meanwhile, Uber and Lyft extract 25–40% of every fare, provide zero ownership, zero benefits, and zero community investment. Drivers are reduced to components in a financial optimization engine. This is the exact opposite of "For Human by Human."

Prosper Drive is our answer. A cooperative where drivers are co-owners, earnings stay in communities, and the platform exists to serve people, not shareholders.

The "For Human by Human" Ride-Hailing Model

We believe a ride-hailing platform can be designed to protect worker dignity, human relationships, and real community value. Not by tweaking Uber's model at the margins, but by replacing the ownership structure, the incentives, and the purpose entirely. When drivers own the platform, everything changes.

How Prosper Drive Is Different
Worker Ownership

Drivers are co-owners of the cooperative. Profits are distributed to the workers who generate them, not extracted to shareholders.

Lower Commissions

Platform takes only what's needed to operate and reinvest. No extraction logic. No quarterly targets. Just fair economics for fair work.

Community Investment

Surplus stays in the communities where it's earned. Local drivers, local riders, local prosperity. Not funneled to Silicon Valley.

Human Relationships

Platform design that values the driver-rider relationship as human interaction, not anonymous transactions to be optimized away.

Transparent Governance

Driver-owners vote on platform decisions. No black-box algorithms. No unilateral pricing changes. Real democratic governance.

Declaration

The Guarantees We Uphold
for our products, solutions, and sovereign technology.

Our Declaration is structured in three levels, each one broader in scope than the last. Level 1 are the concrete promises we make to you as a user or customer. Level 2 are our commitments to the societies and world we all depend on. Level 3 is the structural proof that the first two aren't just words; it describes the ownership model that enforces our values by design. This declaration specifically governs our sovereign technology pillar and the products built under it, rather than replacing the broader community and economic mission of the Foundation.

Level 1: The Guarantees We Uphold

No Lock-In, No Lock-Out. Your data is yours. We guarantee full interoperability, data portability, and complete ownership and control of your information. If you choose to leave, nothing stands in your way. If you're coming from another solution, we support open standards to make bringing your data in just as seamless.

Privacy and Data Ownership Are Fundamental Rights. You will always have full control over who can see your data and why. We offer the option to fully encrypt personal data (files, emails, documents, and communications) so that neither we as your service provider, nor any third party or government, can access it.

Data Minimalism, Not Data Exploitation. We will never collect, request, or mine data beyond what is genuinely needed to deliver a great service. Our customers and users are human beings, not exploitable sources of data.

Healthy Design, Always. You will never encounter manipulative dark patterns designed to hijack your attention, foster addiction, distort perception, or cause mental harm.

Quality Without Compromise. We will never degrade the quality of any of our solutions to increase profit or to serve priorities unrelated to the solution's purpose, unless compelled by circumstances genuinely beyond our control.

Level 2: Our Commitment to the Society and World We All Depend On

Purpose Beyond Profit. We take care and pride in building solutions intended to make the world better, not solely to generate revenue.

People, Not Just Users. We care about the lives and wellbeing of our customers and users beyond the transactional interactions they have with us.

Own Our Impact. We are committed to minimizing negative externalities from our operations, product decisions, and supply chains.

Wider Responsibility. We care about the societies we operate in and the health of the planet.

Level 3: Foundation ownership and rejection of private excess

The third level changes who benefits from success and rejects the ownership logic that normally drives platform extraction. Significant profits from operations do not flow to an elite group of private owners, but to a public-benefit charity or foundation; in ProsperApps' case, the Prosper Foundation.

No corporate excesses or absurd executive compensation. People should be paid well for responsibility, competence, and contribution, but not funded into private-jet and megayacht absurdity.

A long-horizon operating model where mission integrity matters more than maximizing short-term extraction from customers and users.

Economic Liberation

Breaking free from the
payroll-to-payroll trap.

This is our most ambitious pillar and our longest horizon. Economic liberation at scale is generational work. We are not claiming to solve it. We are committing to building toward it, one initiative and one community at a time.

We'll be honest: this is the hardest thing we're doing.

Community Revival can start tomorrow. Neighbors can meet face to face, share a meal, and begin building something real before the week is out. Sovereign Technology is being built now and already operating in alpha. Economic liberation, true sovereignty from payroll dependency at community scale, is a mid-to-long term goal. It requires changing deeply ingrained habits, building skills most people were never taught, and creating new economic structures where none currently exist. We know this. That is why we are starting now, with concrete first steps, rather than waiting until we have a complete theory of change.

Why It Matters

Billions of people are one crisis away from financial collapse.

→AI automation is eliminating millions of jobs, with no guarantee of replacement.
→Gig platforms extract up to 40% of worker earnings while providing zero ownership.
→Most people have one income stream, with no safety net when it disappears.
→Economic dependency is not just financial. It shapes what you can say, do, and become.

The prevailing model tells people to get a good job, stay loyal, and hope the payroll keeps coming. For generations, that worked well enough. It no longer does.

We believe economic sovereignty is a prerequisite for human dignity. When your income depends entirely on a single employer or a single platform, you are not free. You are compliant. And compliance under economic pressure is one of the most powerful forces keeping communities fragile.

Economic Liberation is the second pillar of Prosper Foundation's work because no community can be truly resilient when its members are economically trapped.

The outcome when the other two pillars work together. Community creates the relationships. Technology removes the extractive middlemen. Economic liberation is what happens when people have both: a community of mutual support and tools that do not take a cut of their livelihood.

Pillar 03

Sovereign Technology

ProsperApps was not built only for privacy. Every app in the ecosystem is designed to help practitioners, cooperatives, and small operators run their livelihoods without depending on platforms that extract from them. ProsperPay keeps more money with the worker. ProsperCalendar replaces a booking platform that takes a cut. Sovereign technology is economic liberation infrastructure.

Community Revival

Rebuilding the local ties that keep families human.

We believe the answer to screen dependence, AI companionship, and social pressure is not more isolation; it is stronger face-to-face local life.

Why It Matters

Children need real people, not only optimized engagement.

Children are spending more of their lives with tablets, game consoles, social media, and now AI agents that are designed to keep them engaged and never disagree. That may feel convenient, but it quietly removes the practice of handling conflict, patience, and real human friction.

At the same time, reading ability is declining, social and psychological patterns are shifting, and parents are under heavy pressure to buy phones and accept constant connectivity so their children are not left out at school.

Our response

We believe families should build local, face-to-face life circles in their own apartments, neighborhoods, towns, and cities, spending time together in nature, through sport, and in technology-free spaces where children and adults can learn how to live with one another again.

What Community Revival Means

Face-to-Face Belonging

Regular in-person contact that gives children and adults real social practice instead of only mediated interactions.

Low-Tech Shared Life

Spaces and routines that reduce dependence on phones, AI companions, and constant online attention.

Local Resilience

Families and neighbors supporting each other through shared time, shared responsibility, and shared presence.

Accreditation

The Prosper Accreditation
Program.

A formal recognition for organizations that adopt Prosper's Technology Sovereignty Declaration, committing to the same standards of privacy, data ownership, and human-centered design that we hold ourselves to.

What It Is

A seal that means something.

→Independently verified compliance with the Prosper Declaration
→Formally recognized and listed by Prosper Foundation
→Right to display the Prosper Accredited seal publicly
→Ongoing compliance monitored and enforced by Prosper

The Prosper Accreditation Program exists because declarations without accountability are just marketing. We believe organizations that genuinely uphold the principles of technology sovereignty, data ownership, and human-centered design should be able to demonstrate that in a way visitors and partners can trust.

Accredited organizations have signed a Memorandum of Understanding with Prosper Foundation, formally adopting our Technology Sovereignty Declaration and granting Prosper the right to verify and enforce compliance. In return, they earn the right to publish the Prosper Accredited Seal on their websites and to be publicly listed here.

How Accreditation Works

Step 1: Adopt the Declaration. The organization reviews and commits to Prosper's Technology Sovereignty Declaration: the standards around privacy, data ownership, portability, healthy design, and purpose beyond profit.

Step 2: Sign the MOU. A formal Memorandum of Understanding is signed with Prosper Foundation, granting us the right and responsibility to verify compliance over time.

Step 3: Earn the Seal. Accredited organizations may publish the Prosper Accredited Seal on their websites and are listed on this page as recognized members of the accreditation program.

Ongoing: Compliance Enforcement. Prosper Foundation monitors compliance. Accreditation is not a one-time checkmark; it is a maintained commitment. Organizations that fall out of compliance are delisted.

Two Paths to Accreditation
Path 01

Declaration-Based Accreditation

Any organization, regardless of what technology they use, may apply for accreditation by formally adopting the Prosper Technology Sovereignty Declaration and signing an MOU. Prosper reviews and verifies compliance on an ongoing basis.

Path 02

Automatic Accreditation

Organizations building on the ProsperApps platform are automatically eligible for accreditation. Because ProsperApps is structurally designed to comply with the Declaration, organizations using it inherit compliance by design, not just by promise.

The Prosper Accredited Seal

A mark the public can rely on

The Prosper Accredited Seal is not a self-reported badge. It is issued by Prosper Foundation only to organizations that have formally adopted the Declaration, signed an MOU, and passed our compliance review. When you see it on an organization's website, you can trust that Prosper stands behind that claim, and that we will remove it if the organization no longer meets the standard.

The seal name and visual identity are currently in development. If you have ideas or would like to be involved in shaping the program, reach out through the Get Involved page.

Accredited Organizations

Program launching soon

The Prosper Accreditation Program is in its design phase. The first accredited organizations will be listed here once the MOU framework and compliance review process are formalized. Organizations interested in early participation are encouraged to reach out.
